Learn more about anxiety, depression, and what to do if you need help.

Anxiety

A mental health disorder characterized by feelings of worry, anxiety, or fear that are strong enough to interfere with one's daily activities Examples of anxiety disorders include panic attacks, obsessive-compulsive disorder, and post-traumatic stress disorder. Symptoms include stress that's out of proportion to the impact of the event, inability to set aside a worry, and restlessness. Treatment includes counseling or medications, including antidepressants.

Depression

A mental health disorder characterized by persistently depressed mood or loss of interest in activities, causing significant impairment in daily life. Possible causes include a combination of biological, psychological, and social sources of distress. You may feel sad for a long period of time or lose interest in things that you normally love doing, both of which are the most well known symptoms of depression. This can lead to many other behavioral or physical symptoms, including changes in sleep, appetit, energy level, concentration, daily behavior, or self-esteem. Depression can also be associated with thoughts of suicide.

Our Quiz

Often times, people don't know if they show symptoms of mental health issues, and the high cost of healthcare can also make it seem like it's not worth it to go. This quiz is by no means a complete diagnoses, but rather a starting point so that people can feel that when they go to their appointment, they really should go to one.

What's the next step?

Since we are not healthcare professionals, the next step is to get into contact with a licensed professional. There, they can really diagnose you for a mental health issue, and they can make you feel better by giving you the right treatments.
